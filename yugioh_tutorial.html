Yugioh Tutorial: The Basics

Ruleset

The Yugioh Ruleset consists of these basic principles

  • There are two players, each with 8000 life points (LP for short) this is their Health bar, when it reaches zero that player will lose
  • Each player starts with 5 cards
  • There are 2 decks for each player their main deck which consists of normal,effect,spell and trap cards which can go up to a max of 60 and a minimum of 40 cards
  • There is the extra deck which has a minimum of 0 and max of 15 cards, this consists of only monsters those being Fusion,Synchro,Xyz, and Link.
  • If a player runs out of cards to draw, they will also lose the duel
  • The game consists of 6 phases, draw,standby,main,battle,main 2, and end
    • The draw phase is where the player draws one card from their main deck, the player who plays the first turn of the game does not draw in their draw phase
    • The standby phase is where nothing happens unless a cards effect activates during that phase
    • The main phase is where the player summons monsters, activates spells and traps to allow the player to employ their straegy
    • The battle phase is where the player commands their monsters to attack the other player, however the first of the game the battle phase does not happen
      • When battling, the monster with the higher attacks will win always when attacking a monster with a lower attack/defense, only when a monster is attacked in attack mode will a player take damage
      • When an attacking monster attacks a monster with a higher defense then the attacking will take damage but niether card will be destoryed
      • When attacking a monster with the same attack, both monsters will be destoyed
      • When the attacking player has no monster to attack, they will attack the player directly for their LP
    • The main phase 2 is the exact same as main phase 1
    • The end phase is where the player ends their turn, cards effect that require the end phase activate here
  • You can summon is various ways, the main two are normal and special summons
    • Normal summoning consists of player placing a card on the field from their hand only, it can only be once per turn
    • Special summoning consists of a player placing card on the field from their hand and deck depending on a cards effect, it can be done as many times per turn
    • Flip summoning is where when a monster card face down is flipped face up, this is usally done to activate an effect
  • There are 2 places can a card can go depending on how the card is removed, banishment zone, and the graveyard
    • The graveyard is where a card goes after being destoryed or used, effects that have graveyard requirements can be used there
    • The banishment zone is where a card goes after being banished, it similar to the graveyard effects can be used here, however cards can be placed face down making them unplayable
  • The field consists of zones, 5 for normal and effect monsters, 5 for spells and traps (2 of which pendulum monsters go on), 1 for a field spell, 1 for only an extra deck monsters

Types of Yugioh Monsters

There are various type of monsters each with their own level from 1-12 and each with their own attack and defense

  • Normal
    • Normal Monsters are the basic monsters with no special effects and their text below is just a description of the card itself
  • Effect
    • Effect Monsters are monsters with special effects with certain conditions to be met, unlike normal monsters they have no description of the card itself
  • Pendulum
    • Pendulum Monsters are similar to effect monsters, the main different is that they have their own effects called pendulum effects
    • The Pendulum effects activate when they in the pendulum zones (which is in the spell/trap zones each to the far right and left)
    • Each pendulum monster has a scale going from 0-12
    • Depending on the range the of the two pendulum monsters pendulum cards can be pendulum summoned EX:One side is 1 and other is 3, only level 2 pendulum monsters can be summoned from the hand
    • Pendulum can also be Fusion, Synchro and XYZ Monsters
  • Fusion
    • Fusion Monsters are normal and effect monsters that need two or more monsters and a fusion spell/monster effect card in order to be summoned, this is called a Fusion Summononing
    • Fusion Monsters have certain requirements in order to be sommoned, usually being a specific type of monster
    • All cards used for the summon normally go to the graveyard or banishment zone depending on the cards effect
  • Synchro
    • Synchro Monsters are normal and effect monsters that two or more monsters but instead a tuner monster in order to be summoned, this is called Synchro Summoning
    • Synchro Monsters also have different types of monsters that are always needed, the need that always consistent is that a tuner and a non tuner is needed
  • XYZ
    • XYZ Monsters are normal and effect monsters that need two or more monsters however these two monsters must be the exact same level in order to be summoned, this is called XYZ Summoning
    • Similar to Fusion and Synchro all cards have different requirements
    • The cards don't immediately need go to graveyard, instead you stack the cards on top of eachother with the XYZ monster on top
    • In order to use their effects they must remove their at least one of "materials" (the cards used for the summon)
    • Usually the only way to gain more material is via a spell card or a monsters effects
  • Link
    • Link monsters are normal and effect monsters that need 1 or monsters, any monster can be used for the summon as long as they meet the requirments, this is called Link summoning
    • Link monsters are unique in that they do not have a defense value so they can only be put in attack mode
    • Link monsters have arrows that can point in any direction, if any arrow connects with another they are linked some monsters benefit from being linked via their effects
    • Initially when first summoning them they can only be in the extra monster zone and any other link monster can be summon to a zone a link is pointing to, except the opponents side of the field

Yugioh Spell and Trap Cards

Yugioh Spell and Traps have a variety of effects, some made for certain monsters

  • Spell Cards
    • Spell Cards are cards with affects that help the player employ a strategy
    • Spell Cards can be placed face down and activated immediately as long as the requirements are met
    • There are Coninous Spell Cards that last the who game unless removed or destoryed
    • There are Quick Play Spell cards that can be played during the opponents turn in order to counter their strategy
    • There are Field Spell cards that can be played in the field spell zone that are similar to continuous spell cards
    • There are Equip Spell cards which when activated enhances a monsters attack/defense or giving it an effect
  • Trap Cards
    • Trap cards are similar to spell cards however they must be placed face down for at least until the end of that players turn
    • Trap cards can be used always during an opponents turn in order to stop their strategy
    • There are continuous Trap Cards, which when activated last the entire game unless removed or destroyed
    • There are Counter trap cards that can only be activated in response to another players actions
